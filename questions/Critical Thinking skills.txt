1. Which of the following is NOT a characteristic of critical thinking?
a. Open-mindedness
b. Skepticism
c. Dogmatism
d. Flexibility

2. True or False: Critical thinking involves accepting information without questioning it.
a. True
b. False

3. Which of the following is NOT a way to improve your critical thinking skills?
a. Asking clarifying questions
b. Seeking out multiple sources of information
c. Accepting information at face value
d. Considering different perspectives

4. True or False: Critical thinking always leads to the same conclusions.
a. True
b. False

5. Which of the following is NOT a way to evaluate the credibility of a source?
a. Checking the author's credentials
b. Looking for bias
c. Examining the evidence supporting the claims
d. Ignoring the date of publication

6. True or False: It is important to suspend judgment while engaging in critical thinking.
a. True
b. False

7. Which of the following is NOT a way to apply critical thinking in your daily life?
a. Evaluating the credibility of news sources
b. Making informed decisions about healthcare
c. Analyzing advertising techniques
d. Ignoring the consequences of your actions

8. True or False: Critical thinking always involves being critical of others.
a. True
b. False

9. Which of the following is NOT a benefit of critical thinking?
a. Improved problem-solving skills
b. Greater close-mindedness
c. Enhanced communication skills
d. Increased creativity

10. True or False: It is important to challenge your own beliefs and assumptions when engaging in critical thinking.
a. True
b. False

ANSWERS
1. c. Dogmatism
2. a. True
3. c. Accepting information at face value
4. b. False
5. d. Ignoring the date of publication
6. a. True
7. d. Ignoring the consequences of your actions
8. b. False
9. b. Greater close-mindedness
10. a. True
